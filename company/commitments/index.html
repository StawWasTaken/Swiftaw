The promises we keep

What Swiftaw commits to.

These aren't marketing lines. These are the constraints our team writes code inside of.

1Your data is yours

We never sell user data. We never train on private data. We support full export and deletion on demand — all the way to raw backups.

2Honest incident reports

When something breaks or leaks, we publish what happened, when, and what changed — within 7 days. No "security incident" with no details.

3No dark patterns

Unsubscribe links actually unsubscribe. Cancellation takes two clicks. No confirmshaming, no fake urgency, no hidden toggles.

4Open, inspectable crypto

Every cryptographic choice we make is documented and — where feasible — open-sourced for review. No rolled-our-own-crypto we can't defend.

5Plain-language terms

Our terms of service and privacy policy read like English. If it takes a lawyer to decode, we rewrite it.

6Long-term stability

We commit to multi-year support windows for public APIs, and we publish deprecation timelines long before we act on them.

7Employees over investors

We stay independent. No board seats traded for funding that would compromise product decisions.

8Public mistakes, public fixes

When we get a design wrong, we say so — and we publish the rework. No quiet rollbacks, no disappearing docs.

The only thing we really sell is trust. Every line of these commitments exists to protect that.

— The Swiftaw team

Swiftaw is an independent research-led technology company building a single ecosystem of trustworthy tools.
