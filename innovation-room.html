the gist

right. so. stampled is the first weird thing growing legs inside the Lab. we've been quietly poking at it for a few months now and it's starting to look like something we can show people.

short version: a new way to put your mark on stuff online. not a like. not a heart. not a thumbs-up. something with a bit more weight to it. something you actually carry around with you between rooms.

some ideas deserve more than a like button. - the whole reason we started

what we're sure about

  • stamps are personal. you collect them the way you'd collect stickers, patches, pins, whatever your thing is. a small inventory that's yours.
  • stamps mean something. they're tied to where you've been, what you've made, who you've stamped, who's stamped you back.
  • stamps are quiet. nothing about them goes algorithm-shaped. no leaderboards. no streaks. no public ranking of who's worth more.
  • stamps are public when you want them. private when you don't. the toggle is one tap and it actually does what it says.

what we're not sure about

basically everything else. how you find new stamps. whether stamps should expire. whether you should be able to forge them. whether the wax seal should be skeuomorphic or flat (the team is split, it's getting tense).

we'll figure it out. or we won't, and it'll quietly become idea 048 on the wall up top. either way.

field notes from this week

✅ what works

the collection metaphor. people get it in under five seconds and immediately start asking which stamps they can get next.

⚠️ what's broken

the way you discover new stamps. still a mess. currently it's basically just vibes and luck.

👻 what's haunted

clicking the stamp button sometimes plays a chime from 2003. no idea where it came from. we kind of like it.

still early. still weird. still changing every week. that's usually a good sign.
